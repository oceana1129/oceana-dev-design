Bobs Boba

Bubble tea brand brought to life through expressive web design

This project was a personal design exercise focused on translating a hand-drawn brand identity into a joyful and functional e-commerce website. The goal was to craft a visually rich user experience that felt inviting, joyful, and easy to navigate.

Role

UX/UI Designer

Timeframe

1 week

Skills

Visual Design, Branding, Prototyping, e-commerce Design

Learn more about the project

↓

How can I design a bubble tea website that feels unique, handmade, and artistic, while still being clear and easy to shop? ✦

Challenge

This fictional brand imagines two roommates starting a cozy boba tea business. The brand is casual, creative, and expressive. I set out to design a website that feels like a dip into their world. Something that would inspire thoughts of fun, creativity, and warmth.

Goals

  • Build a cohesive visual identity using illustrations and branding.
  • Create a clear layout for browsing drinks and learning about the brand.
  • Highlight storytelling and emotional tone throughout the interface.

Branding

Everything was designed with a hand-drawn aesthetic to give the site a personalized feel. Everything was made intentionally imperfect to add personality and charm.

Illustrations

Illustrations were kept simple, bold, and fluid. Thick and choppy lines were used. Colors flow outside the lines to reinforce a hand-made look. Rounded, asymmetrical shapes were used to add playfulness. Gradients were used sparingly to imply depth.

Each drink is hand-drawn to ensure each drink feels personalized and unique.

Colors

A soft and vibrant palette was used to reflect the tone of the brand. Peaches, creams, and blushes create warmth while incorporating a subtle visual nod to boba milk drinks. While bright pinks, reds, and yellows add contrast and bring visual energy.

Style

A typography style was used to match the hand-drawn style of the branding, whilst being rounded, friendly, and legible. When paired with the illustrations, color palette, and icons, the brand now echoes a design language that is fun, flavorful, and full of personality.

Bobs Boba In Action

Landing Page

Welcome the user, set the tone, and guide exploration

The homepage introduces the brand with bold, hand-drawn illustrations and playful layouts. Clear calls to action encourage users to explore the shop or learn more about the story.

Menus

Have fun browsing drink options

Users can scroll through categories of items: milk teas, fruit teas, and merchandise. Each item in the shop is individually hand-drawn, paired with quirky descriptions and bubbly animations. Providing a delightful and intuitive browsing experience.

Our Story

Building emotional connections

The page tells the brand founders' story and their love for boba tea. Illustrations paired with the brand values help to create trust and personality through visual storytelling.

Contact Form

Encouraging users to connect

A soft, minimalistic contact form lets users send out any questions and concerns. Previous visual and decorative elements keep the page feeling friendly and aligned with the brand.

Drink Easter Eggs

A little novelty to keep users highly engaged

Using a simple functionality, a rotating carousel of drinks can be clicked on in the home page and lead our users to the corresponding drink. A simple way to keep users engaged while connecting them to the brand.

Future Improvements

The purpose of Bobs Boba was to act as a design exercise to see how far visual storytelling could be used in an e-commerce setting. The timeline was short to help me make design decisions with expediency and efficiency. The website was also coded by myself, which adds to the time I had for the project.

However, with that in mind, if I had more time, what elements would I add to flesh out the concept even more?

  • Cart and checkout design flow: using illustrated icons and visual flow to keep the user engaged as they order an item.
  • More microinteractions: adding more subtle animations for hover and click transitions.
  • Mobile view: currently, the website is incomplete as it only supports desktop and tablet views.
  • Accessibility support: running accessibility reports to ensure color contrast, keyboard navigation, and screen reader support are in place.

Key Takeaways

The project was a chance to explore how far visual storytelling could be used in an e-commerce setting, while making the user experience clear and joyful. I learned some very integral lessons in this short project.

  • Illustrations can be powerful UX tools when paired with thoughtful layout decisions.
  • Visual storytelling reflects brand vision and keeps users engaged beyond just functionality.
  • Playful designs need a strong visual hierarchy to stay accessible and avoid overwhelming users.

One of the most rewarding outcomes of this design exercise was the reactions of those who interacted with the site. Several people asked if Bobs Boba was a real client, and expressed disappointment that the shop didn’t actually exist. This kind of emotional response showed me how even a simple design concept, when crafted with care and attention, can create a strong and meaningful impact.
