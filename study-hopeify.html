How can we create a mental health platform that lets users schedule sessions, explore resources, and take assessments effortlessly? ✦

Challenge

Hopeify is a mental health platform built by a team of five core members with experience in digital mental health. Our goal was to design a modern, AI-powered platform that integrates therapy, self-guided programs, and assessments—to provide support, particularly for individuals with chronic illnesses.

When we started, Hopeify had a vision but little groundwork. My task was to research, define key features, and design a web application from scratch. The initial MVP needed to be easy to develop with limited funds.

Goals

  • Design a user-friendly and accessible mental health platform.
  • Integrate AI-driven assessments and human-led care seamlessly.
  • Prioritize accessibility across different user needs.

Research

The need for mental health support online is abundantly clear. Around 1 in 5 U.S. adults live with a mental illness. The delay between mental health onset and treatment is around 11 years. And globally, it is estimated that 160 million people live in a designate Mental Health Professional Shortage area.

Many individuals suffer with mental health needs are are unable to get access to adequate resources.

Key Takeaways

Hopeify needed to fill critical gaps in the digital mental health space by:

  • Combining therapy & self-guided resources into a holistic platform.
  • Prioritizing accessibility for individuals with disabilities.
  • Creating AI-driven personalization to help users navigate their mental health.
  • Developing specific support for chronic illness.

User Journey

To develop the platform, it’s important to understand the user journey. How does a user discover Hopeify and how will they stay from long term engagement. Identifying pain points and opportunities help us refine the platform.

Ultimately, we want to make sure the platform is intuitive, accessible, and effective.

User Flow

Since Hopeify handles many different aspects, the user flow must be intuitive and easy to navigate. Whether seeking therapy, taking an assessment, or accessing resources, the platform must utilize clear entry points and transitions.

Wireframing

Before building the prototype, a low fidelity wireframe was created to help understand the flow of Hopeify. From the landing page, login, sign up, onboard, dashboard, finding a provider, setting up a session, to taking an assessment.

Welcome Home

“Welcome Home.”

This was the guiding principle for Hopeify’s branding. We wanted users to feel welcomed, calm, and supported when they engage with the platform. Unlike other mental health competitors, whose branding feels too clinical or generic, we aimed to establish an identity that was calm and trusting, distinctive, professional, but not clinical.

Logo

Hopeify’s logo underwent multiple iterations to capture hope, support, and compassion. We explored various concepts from hearts, hugs, flowers, suns, before honing in on a design representing care and emotional security.

Colors

For the colors, we went through a few different iterations. We ended up deliberating between a blue and green color scheme and ultimately decided on blue.

Style

After going through several iterations, we decided on branding that could foster the idea “Welcome Home.”

User Testing

Given the large scope of this project, we conducted multiple rounds of user testing to refine the experience. Two major areas of feedback led to key iterations:

Onboarding Flow Clarity

Users felt uncertain about their next steps after signing up, leading to confusion and frustration.

Solution:

  • I redesigned the onboarding flow to provide clearer guidance.
  • Added progress indicators and visual cues to aid users.
  • Incorporated AI companion feedback to help provide additional clarity.

AI Companion Engagement

Users found AI companion, ANDI, unnecessary or were hesitant to engage with it.

Solution:

  • Introduced ANDI early in the flow to establish familiarity.
  • Ensured ANDI played a key role in providing personalized recommendations.
  • Enhanced the visual appeal of ANDI’s avatar and interactions to increase engagement.

Next Steps

Hopeify is an ongoing project, while my primary focus has been refining the patient experience, I’m now shifting my attention to optimize the provider and superuser interfaces.

With funding secured, we are moving into MVP development. During this process, my focus will be on refining our existing work to aid development. Later I’ll be gathering further insights through user testing.

Future Improvements

Areas I plan to enhance include:

  • Accessibility Design: ensuring users with various disabilities can navigate the platform seamlessly or have the necessary tools to do so.
  • Direct-to-Consumer Flow: while much of the project focused on B2C, the D2C journey needs further refinement.
  • Dashboard Optimization: striking a balance between providing valuable information without overwhelming users, particularly on the dashboard.

What I Learned

This was my first large-scale project, and it pushed my skills to the limit. At the start, we had almost nothing—just a few Canva concepts, a logo, and vague guidelines.

One of the biggest challenges was building a prototype that wasn’t just for development, but also for securing funding. This meant defining what was essential for the MVP and what should wait for future stages. Some features, like a journal system I designed, had to be scrapped along the way.

I also learned that not every design decision is in my control. While I aimed to avoid bloat, I had to adapt when asked to design elements I felt were unnecessary. This experience taught me to work within complex systems and balance design priorities.

Resources were also limited. In an ideal world, I would have conducted broader research and user testing, but working with constraints helped me prioritize insights and make efficient decisions.

Most importantly, I learned the value of long-term collaboration. I worked with an incredible team, even mentored a designer. Seeing an idea evolve into a tangible product through persistence and teamwork has been incredibly rewarding.
