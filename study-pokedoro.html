Pokedoro

A Pokemon productivity app that turns work sessions into an adventure

Pokedoro is a web application that gamifies productivity with Pokemon. Users track focus sessions, train their Pokémon, and earn rewards by completing Pomodoro cycles.

In this project I led the visual identity, front-end design, and interactive UI elements, in a collaborative setting.

Role

UX/UI, Front-End

Timeframe

3 weeks

Skills

Visual Design, Branding, Prototyping, Front End Development

How can I design a web application that’s playful and motivating, while still encouraging users to focus? ✦

Challenge

Pokedoro aimed to balance playful game-inspired visuals with a clean, usable interface. The core challenge was ensuring the experience felt rewarding without distracting from the app’s productivity-centered goal. We wanted users to feel invested, like they were training with their favorite Pokemon, while staying focused.

Goals

  • Create a nostalgic and friendly UI.
  • Guide users with an intuitive layout and storytelling.
  • Make focusing feel like a game.

First Mockups

After some initial research, I worked on sketches to get an idea for how to place elements and work out the flow. From the sketches, I worked out which ideas worked and which didn't. They were also helpful for gathering feedback from my collaborators.

Visual Identity

I designed the interface to evoke classic handheld retro Pokemon games. A dark mode theme was implemented, reminiscent of old CRT screens, enhanced with subtle glows and contrast. I used pixel style icons, monospace and bitmap fonts, and rounded UI elements to blend the modern with retro pixel graphics.

Colors

I designed a personalization feature incorporating a dashboard color scheme change based on the user’s chosen Pokedoro buddy, to increase emotional investment.

Pokedoro In Action

Sign Up Flow

Guides the user and create personal investment

The landing page briefly introduces the concept. Upon signing up, users are immersed into the productivity app by a warm introduction from a Pokedoro Professor. Users personalize their journey by selecting a profile avatar and starter Pokemon.

Dashboard

Train your Pokedoro buddy through Pomodoro sessions

The core of the app. Users choose a session (Focus, Break, Rest), and their Pokemon reacts accordingly. When a new buddy is selected, the entire interface adapts visually. Integrated features like a lo-fi music player, Pokemon capture tool, and real-time updates keep users engaged and grounded.

Pokemon Collection

Lets users browse, sort, and manage their Pokemon

This grid layout lets users view all caught Pokemon, sorted by level, type, or rarity. Clicking a sprite opens a modal to show the evolution stage, XP, and options to release or set the Pokemon as your Pomodoro buddy.

Profile Settings

A personal way to reflect on user progress

The profile page displays the users trainer card: profile icon, total unique Pokemon collected, time focused, score, and current training buddy. There are also additional toggles to enable sound effects and notifications. Users can delete their profiles from here.

Future Improvements

While Pokedoro was built on a tight timeline, we received great feedback and saw room to expand the concept even further. If given more time, I would have loved to refine:

  • User testing: validate flow and uncover confusing interactions.
  • Improved gamification: more meaningful progress systems, more badges, and animations.
  • Audio feedback: custom sounds for capture, evolution, and timer states.
  • Tutorial: a short post onboarding guide to improve comprehension and reduce drop-off.

Key Takeaways

I really enjoyed this project, and it presented interesting challenges to me in terms of design. It was a great opportunity to explore how nostalgia, retro design, and UX can merge to create an application. My team and I received a lot of positive feedback on this project. This project could not have been made without the help of my team members!
